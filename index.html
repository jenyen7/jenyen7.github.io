Leopard Cat

The leopard cat is a wild cat, having the size of a domestic cat with leopard fur pattern, mostly found in South, Southeast and East Asia.

Distribution


Figure 1. Distribution map

As shown in the map above, they are mainly distributed in Asia, west from Pakistan to far east of Russia.

These leaopard cats live in wide range of habitats, including tropical evergreen rainforests, subtropical deciduous forests, coniferous forests and even in human-modified environments.


Behaviour and Diet

These solitary cats are active during the day but mostly hunt at night. They are agile climbers and swimmers, feeding on small preys, such as small mammals, fishes, birds, amphibians and insects.



Reproduction

In tropical regions, there is no specific breeding season for the leopard cats but in colder regions, they reproduce during the spring season, when the weather is warm enough for the kittens to be able to survive.
Their gestation period takes 60 to 70 days with approximately two to four kittens per litter.



Threats and Conservation

Leopard cats are hunted mainly because of their fur and are now considered as endangered species in Japan and the United States.
